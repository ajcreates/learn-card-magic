## Overview

This is a showstopper. A spectator chooses a card — completely freely — and it vanishes from the deck only to be found inside a sealed envelope you placed on the table *before the trick began*.

This effect combines a **force**, a **control**, and a **classic card to envelope** climax.

## What You'll Need

- A standard deck
- A sealed envelope with a duplicate card inside
- (Optional) A card to wallet for the ultimate version

## The Method

This trick relies on forcing a specific card on the spectator. You'll need one of these forces:
- **Classic Force** (advanced but most deceptive)
- **Cross-cut Force** (easiest, highly reliable)

### The Setup
Seal a card (let's use the 7 of Hearts) in an envelope. Place it on the table in plain sight before you begin.

### The Cross-Cut Force

1. Have the spectator cut the deck anywhere and place their half on the table
2. Pick up the other half and place it cross-wise on top
3. Gesture at the envelope and talk briefly to distract attention
4. Lift off the top (cross) packet and say "Remember where you cut…"
5. Show the card at the face of the bottom packet — this is actually the card that *was* on top (your force card, 7♥)

The spectator believes they freely chose where to cut. In fact, you're showing them a pre-arranged card.

### The Revelation
Have them look at their card. Build suspense. Point to the envelope that's been in view the whole time. Have them open it — it's their card.

## Performing Tips

- The cross-cut force requires a brief time misdirection. Keep talking about the envelope.
- Pre-show: let spectators inspect the envelope. They'll never suspect the card is already inside.
- Practice the force 20x before performing.

## Upgrade: Card to Wallet

For close-up performances, the card to wallet is even more stunning — their signed card appears in your wallet, which has been in your back pocket the entire time.
