## Overview

The Ambitious Card is considered one of the greatest card tricks ever invented. A spectator selects a card, it gets buried in the middle of the deck — and impossibly rises back to the top. Again and again.

It requires no difficult sleight of hand, making it the perfect first trick to learn.

## What You'll Need

- A standard deck of 52 playing cards
- 10–15 minutes of practice

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: The Setup
Before you begin, secretly note the top card of your deck. This is your **key card**.

### Step 2: The Selection
Fan the cards face-down and ask your spectator to select a card. Have them remember it and show it to the audience.

### Step 3: The Control
Ask the spectator to place their card back anywhere in the middle. Perform a simple **overhand shuffle**, controlling their card to the top.

> **Tip:** The overhand control is simple — as the card goes in, undercut everything below it to the top in one smooth motion.

### Step 4: The Revelation
Snap your fingers over the deck and flip over the top card — it's their card!

### Step 5: Repeat (The Ambitious Phase)
Bury the card in the middle again. Snap. It's on top. Do this 3–4 times, each time with a different "magical gesture" to add variety.

### Step 6: The Kicker Ending
For the final phase, visibly push the card face-up into the middle of the face-down deck. Snap — it not only rises to the top, but flips itself face-up while the rest remain face-down.

## Practice Tips

- Practice the overhand control slowly at first until it looks natural
- Your patter (what you say) matters as much as the moves
- Build up the drama on each repetition — the third time should feel impossible
