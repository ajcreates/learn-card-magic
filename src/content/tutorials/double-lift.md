## Overview

If there's one technique every card magician must master, it's the **Double Lift**. You turn over two cards as if they were one, creating the illusion of a single card. It's the foundation for dozens of classic effects.

## What You'll Need

- A standard deck of cards
- Patience — this takes practice to look natural

## Understanding the Double Lift

The Double Lift works because the spectator has no reason to suspect two cards are being turned. The key is making it look **exactly** like a single card turnover.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: The Break
Rest the deck in your left hand. With your right thumb, riffle the top two cards slightly at the back corner. Catch a subtle break (gap) below the top two cards.

### Step 2: The Grip
With your right hand, grip the two cards as one — thumb at the back, first finger at the front edge. Keep the cards perfectly squared.

### Step 3: The Turnover
Turn the two cards face-up as one onto the top of the deck. They should land flat and squared. Don't pinch — let them pivot naturally.

### Step 4: The Display
Show the face of the "card" (actually two cards) to the spectator. This is the card they think is on top.

### Step 5: The Replace
Turn the double face-down back onto the deck in the same pivoting motion.

### Step 6: The Use
The top card of the deck is now **different** from what the spectator thinks. You can now deal it face-down, put it in a pocket, or anywhere — they believe it's the card they saw.

## Practice Drill

1. Start with a stiff, new deck — easier to handle
2. Practice the break 50 times without looking
3. Practice the turnover in front of a mirror
4. Try it slowly in front of a friend before adding it to a trick

## Common Mistakes

- **Pinching the cards** — causes them to separate visibly
- **Looking at your hands** — always maintain eye contact with your spectator
- **Rushing** — slow down the turnover; it looks more natural

## Tricks That Use the Double Lift

- Card to Pocket
- Any Ambitious Card routine
- Two-Card Monte
- Oil and Water
